The Era of AI-Native Browsing

May 14, 2026 By Disco Team

Since the dawn of the World Wide Web, the browser has been a window. A passive viewer that renders pixels based on HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. We’ve spent decades perfecting the art of "viewing" content. But with Google Disco, we’re asking a different question: What if the browser was a worker?

Beyond the Tab

Current browsers organize information into tabs. Each tab is an isolated island. If you want to plan a trip, you open ten tabs—flights, hotels, things to do, maps, reviews. You then spend hours manually synthesizing that information into a coherent plan. You are the glue between the data.

"Disco isn't just a browser; it's a synthesis engine that understands your intent across the entire web."

Disco introduces GenTabs (Generative Tabs). When you start a task—say, "Plan a 3-day trip to Tokyo with a focus on street food"—Disco doesn't just open pages. It reads them. It understands them. And then, it builds a custom tool for you right in your browser.

The Synthesis Engine

Powered by Gemini 3, Disco can reason across multiple data sources in real-time. It can identify pricing trends, cross-reference reviews with location data, and even predict availability. It transforms the "search and click" cycle into a "request and receive" experience.

This is the start of AI-native browsing. An era where your tools are as dynamic as the web itself.
